Language Audio Recognition

For my Data Science Immersive capstone, I collected over 125 hours of human speech to identify the language being spoken. Five second clips were transformed into Mel-frequency cepstral coefficients (MFCCs) to best represent the human voice and range of hearing. Using a recurrent neural network, audio was classified as belonging to one of five major world languages: English, Spanish, French, Russian, or Mandarin Chinese.

Reddit Natural Language Processing

Thousands of posts from r/Coffee and r/tea were scraped to form two classes using the Pushshift reddit API. After cleaning, stemming, lemmatization, and stop-word removal, post content was analyzed using 1- and 2-word tokens from count vectorization. The resulting logistic regression model correctly identified the subreddit of 92% of the posts it had not previously seen.

Predicting High School Graduation Rates

Working in a team of three, my colleagues and I used financial data from the National Center for Education Statistics to predict public school districts' 4-year graduation rates. Each state's graduation data is published independently, and had to be individually sourced, imported, and reformatted to match the national spending records. By transforming finances to be on a per-student, log-scaled rate, I was able to increase the R-squared score of the model from under 40% to above 70%.
